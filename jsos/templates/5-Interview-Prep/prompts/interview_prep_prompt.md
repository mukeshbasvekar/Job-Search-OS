## 7.3 Day-Of Game Plan

### 5 Minutes Before
- Review the Quick Reference Card (1.2)
- Re-read the "Why This Company" short version
- Take 3 deep breaths

### During the Interview
- **First 30 seconds:** Warm, confident, human. Smile. Thank them for their time.
- **TMAY:** Use the tailored version. End with a bridge to "why this role."
- **Behavioral questions:** Teaser → STAR → Learning. Keep answers under 2 minutes.
- **Their questions:** Answer the actual question first, then add context.
- **Your questions:** Pick 2–3 from the prepared list based on conversation flow.
- **Close:** Always ask the objection-surfacing question. Express genuine interest.

### After the Interview
- Send a thank-you email within 2 hours
- Note what went well and what to improve
- If multi-round, prepare for the next round based on signals from this one

## Execution Rules

1. **Be specific, not generic.** Every answer, pitch, and question must reference real data about this company and real experience from your background. If an answer could work for any company, rewrite it.

2. **Web search is mandatory.** Use current data for company research. Don't guess revenue, funding, or news — look it up.

3. **Story selection must match JD priorities.** Don't default to favorite stories. Pick stories that prove what THIS role cares about.

4. **Predict, don't just prepare.** The goal is to walk in knowing what they'll ask before they ask it. Use the JD, company culture, and role type to predict specific questions.

5. **Practice-ready format.** Every narrative section (TMAY, Why This Company, Why Me) should be written as spoken text you can rehearse out loud. Not bullet points — actual words you would say.

6. **Honest gap assessment.** If there's a genuine gap between your profile and the role, call it out and provide a mitigation strategy. Don't pretend the gap doesn't exist.

7. **Time-box answers.** Behavioral answers: 90–120 seconds. TMAY: 60–90 seconds. "Why this company": 30–60 seconds. Write answers at these lengths.

8. **Speak like a smart peer.** Confident but conversational. Not corporate-speak, not overly casual. Like explaining your work over coffee.
