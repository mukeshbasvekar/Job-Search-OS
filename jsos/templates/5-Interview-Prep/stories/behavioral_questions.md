# Behavioral Interview Master Doc (MECE)

One file for behavioral prep:
- **32 real questions** (the ones interviewers actually ask)
- **MECE buckets** (instant recognition)
- **Ground rules** (delivery checklist)
- **Story bank** (fill in with YOUR_behavioral_stories.yaml)

---

## Ground Rules (Read Before Every Mock)

- **Reiterate the question back** (1 line): "Got it. You're asking about…"
- **Take 5–10 seconds** to think. Say it out loud: "Let me take 10 seconds."
- **Teaser first**: "This happened at [Company] when… The lesson was…"
- **Keep one spine**: Situation → Decision → Action → Result → Learning
- If you don't have the perfect match, **use the closest bucket match** and say: "Closest example is…"
- **Positive narrative**: no blaming, no whining, no "my team was dumb"
- **Confidence > complexity**: simple words, high clarity
- **Spaced repetition**: rotate stories weekly so they don't go rusty

## The 32 Questions

*(Reference these when filling in the `questions` field of YOUR_behavioral_stories.yaml)*

### Bucket 1: Decision Under Uncertainty (21%)

1. Tell me about a time you had to make a decision with incomplete information.
2. Tell me about a time you had to prioritize between several equally important things.
3. Tell me about a time you had to change course mid-project.
4. Tell me about a time you had to make a trade-off between quality and speed.
5. Tell me about a decision you made that turned out to be wrong. What did you learn?
6. Tell me about a time you had to weigh multiple competing priorities and decide what to do.
7. Tell me about a time you made a decision that others disagreed with. What happened?

### Bucket 2: Influence Without Authority (20%)

8. Tell me about a time you had to get buy-in from someone who didn't report to you.
9. Tell me about a time you influenced a decision you didn't have authority over.
10. Tell me about a time you changed someone's mind.
11. Tell me about a time you had to sell an idea to a skeptical audience.
12. Tell me about a time you got a team to do something they were resistant to.
13. Tell me about a time you drove alignment across cross-functional teams.

### Bucket 3: Ownership / Stepping Up (12%)

14. Tell me about a time you took ownership of something outside your job description.
15. Tell me about a time you identified a problem before anyone else did and fixed it.
16. Tell me about a time you went above and beyond.
17. Tell me about an initiative you started from scratch.
18. Tell me about a time you drove a project from idea to completion.

### Bucket 4: Pressure / Crisis (8%)

19. Tell me about a time you were under significant pressure. How did you handle it?
20. Tell me about a time you had to deliver something with an impossible deadline.
21. Tell me about a time something went wrong and you had to act fast.
22. Tell me about a time you had to manage multiple crises at once.

### Bucket 5: Conflict / Difficult People (8%)

23. Tell me about a time you had a conflict with a colleague or manager.
24. Tell me about a time you had to give difficult feedback.
25. Tell me about a difficult customer or stakeholder you had to work with.
26. Tell me about a time you had to disagree with a decision from leadership.
27. Tell me about a time you had to navigate a difficult team dynamic.

### Bucket 6: Failure & Learning (7%)

28. Tell me about your biggest professional failure.
29. Tell me about a time a project you led didn't go as planned.
30. Tell me about a time you made an assumption that turned out to be wrong.
31. Tell me about a time you received difficult feedback. How did you respond?
32. What's the biggest mistake you've made, and what did you learn?

## Story Variations

Many stories can answer multiple questions. Use this to understand how to angle a story differently:

**Same story, different angle:**
- **Prioritization angle**: Lead with the trade-off decision and the criteria you used
- **Influence angle**: Lead with how you got others on board
- **Ownership angle**: Lead with how you identified the problem before being asked
- **Failure angle**: Lead with what you got wrong and what you learned

Practice telling the same story from 2–3 different angles so you can pivot mid-interview.

## Delivery Checklist

Before each mock:
- [ ] Did I tease the punchline upfront?
- [ ] Is my Situation under 30 seconds?
- [ ] Are my Actions specific (not "we did" — "I did")?
- [ ] Does my Result have a number?
- [ ] Did I land on a learning, not just an outcome?
- [ ] Is the whole thing under 2 minutes?
