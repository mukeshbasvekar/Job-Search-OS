# Job Search Playbook

The strategic framework that runs the search. Built from 3 months of active data and real results.

---

## The Core Principle: Rule of Three

Run three tracks in parallel every day. Not in sequence. Not one at a time. All three, every day.

| Track | Volume | Cadence | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| Track 1: Core list (scored startup shortlist) | 25 emails/day | Daily, morning | High fit + direct founder access = highest conversion |
| Track 2: Opportunistic (found online, interesting) | 5–10 emails/day | As you spot them | Catches companies not in any database |
| Track 3: Big companies (polished, role-specific) | 5 emails/week | Sunday/Monday batch | Lagging pipeline insurance — slower cycles but real |

**Background habits (not counted as tracks):**
- 5 cold applications/day via ATS (hard cap — psychological structure, not pipeline)
- Networking as it naturally comes — no system, no pressure

---

## What the Data Says

Job search funnel by channel (based on real 3-month data):

| Channel | HM+ Rate (meeting with HM or founder) |
|---|---|
| Cold Email + LinkedIn (direct to founder) | 50–65% |
| Networking (warm intros) | 45–55% |
| Cold Applications (ATS) | 35–45% |
| Inbound (job boards) | 25–35% |

**The insight:** Cold email to founders is the highest-leverage activity, full stop. Every hour spent personalizing a cold email outperforms an hour on ATS applications by 2–3x.

The system here automates the personalization with AI so you can run volume and quality at the same time.

---

## Channel Breakdown

### Track 1: Core List (Cold Email, Scored)

**Why it works:** You're emailing pre-filtered, high-fit companies where you're reaching the decision-maker directly. The scoring system ensures every email goes to someone who could actually hire you.

**How to run:**
1. Use `4-Company-Research/` scripts to build your scored shortlist
2. Pick 5 companies from the top of the list each morning (genuine interest, not random)
3. Find founder email (Apollo.io free: 50 credits/month)
4. Generate tailored email with `email_prompt.md` + Claude
5. Send same day
6. Track in `3-Outreach/tracker_template.csv`

**Volume math:**
25 emails/day × 20 work days = 500 emails/month
At 50% reply rate = 250 conversations
At 30% conversion to HM call = 75 calls/month

---

### Track 2: Opportunistic

**Why it works:** The best companies often aren't in any database. They come from reading Twitter/X, LinkedIn, newsletters, or conversations. Opportunistic emails have the highest reply rate because your interest is most genuine.

**How to run:**
- Keep a running list of companies you encounter in daily life that genuinely interest you
- Same process as Track 1, but same-day instead of batched
- No minimum — these are bonus reps

---

### Track 3: Big Companies

**Why it works:** Big companies have longer cycles but more stable processes. They're lagging pipeline — if Track 1 and 2 close fast, great. If not, Track 3 is the safety net.

**How to run:**
- Pick 3 reasons you fit the specific role (not generic "I love the company")
- Send polished, role-specific emails — reference the exact team, product area, or initiative
- Batch on Sundays so they hit inboxes Monday morning
- 5 per week is enough — don't let this cannibalize Track 1

---

## Weekly Metric That Matters

**Target: 2+ HM/Founder calls booked per week.**

Everything else (emails sent, applications submitted) is an activity metric. HM calls booked is the outcome metric. If you're not hitting 2+/week, fix the email (personalization, bullets, subject line) — don't just send more.

---

## Morning Ritual

The sequence that makes the system run:

1. **Anchor moment (10 sec):** Remind yourself why you're doing this — what does getting a job actually unlock? Not the job title. The feeling.
2. **Music on**
3. **Open outreach tool** (Apollo, Gmail, your tracker)
4. **Pick 5 companies** from the shortlist
5. **Send emails** — 25 before noon

The ritual is a forcing function. Without it, Track 1 slips, and the whole system degrades.

---

## Decision Rules

These are locked — don't second-guess them during execution:

- **Cold email is P0.** Everything else is secondary.
- **Startups first** for the active sprint (fast hiring cycles, founder direct access).
- **Big companies as lagging insurance**, not the primary pipeline.
- **No new strategy additions** once you're running. Execute what's locked.
- **If reply rate drops below 30%**, fix the email — don't change the channel.
- **If HM calls < 2/week**, audit the top of the funnel (are you reaching the right people?) and the email itself.

## The Search Timeline Framework

| Week | Focus | Milestone |
|---|---|---|
| Week 1–2 | Setup + first batch | First 100 emails sent, tracker live, scores calibrated |
| Week 3–4 | Volume + iteration | First replies in, email A/B tested, 5+ HM calls |
| Week 5–8 | Pipeline management | Active interview rounds, offers in negotiation |
| Week 9+ | Close | Offer accepted or pipeline refreshed |

Most searches at full volume (Track 1 + 2 + 3) produce meaningful pipeline within 3–4 weeks.

---

## Common Mistakes

- **Optimizing the email instead of sending it.** A B-grade email sent to 500 people beats an A-grade email sent to 50.
- **Waiting for the perfect list.** Start with what you have. The list improves as you send.
- **Tracking vanity metrics.** Emails sent is not the goal. Calls booked is.
- **Pausing the system when interviews start.** Keep Track 1 running even when you have active rounds. Pipeline dries up if you stop.
- **Ignoring the follow-up sequence.** 40–60% of replies come from Email 2 or 3. Set them up automatically.
