Help the user negotiate a job offer using a structured, defensible protocol.

Steps:
1. Read `6-Strategy/NEGOTIATION_PLAYBOOK.md` for the full negotiation framework
2. Ask the user where they are in the process:
   - Have they received a verbal offer, a written offer, or are they being asked for salary expectations before an offer exists?
   - Do they have competing offers or interest elsewhere?
   - Is the offer contingent on anything outside their control (visa transfer, background check, references)?
3. If they only have a verbal offer, the first move is always: tell them to request the full written offer before reacting to anything. Don't negotiate against a phone call.
4. Once the written offer is in hand, walk through the comp breakdown checklist in the playbook (base, bonus/commission structure, equity terms, signing bonus conditions, start date conditions) and flag anything missing or unclear that needs to be asked for in writing.
5. Help them identify the ONE highest-priority ask, not a list of asks, and draft language for it that ties the ask to something defensible (scope, market data, comparable roles) rather than an unanchored number.
6. If they have a visa-dependent start date, apply the Special Case section of the playbook and make sure the user gets the timeline risk terms in writing before accepting.
7. Draft the actual message or talking points they'll use, in their voice, not generic negotiation-speak.
8. Remind them: read the final written offer line by line before signing, and don't renegotiate after accepting.
