# Company Research Template

When given a company name, website URL, and role title — research the company and answer ALL 53 questions below.

Save the output as: `Interviews/companyname_monthyear.md`  
Example: `Interviews/ather_feb2026.md`

---

## Format Rules

- Section name as a heading
- Question number and question as a sub-heading
- Clear, concise answer with specific details, data, and real examples
- **Bold** key numbers, names, and facts for easy scanning
- Use bullet points for clarity
- Be detailed, specific, and structured — NO vague or generic answers

---

## THE PRODUCT

### Competitors

1. Who are the competitors?
2. How does the company differentiate itself from them?

### Customers / Market

3. What is the target market for the company?
4. Are there any secondary markets right now or that you would suggest the company enter?

### Revenue

5. How does the company make money?
6. How would you suggest the company make more money?
7. If it doesn't make money, what revenue strategies would you want to explore?

### Love & Hate

8. How do customers feel about the product?
9. What do they love or hate?
10. What are the most common complaints and issues?

### Metrics

11. What are the company's key metrics?
12. How many users does it have?
13. Which metrics are they doing well on and which ones are they struggling with?
14. What is its conversion rate?
15. What is its growth rate?

### News & Rumors

16. Have there been any interesting news reports about the company?
17. What is the company rumored to do?
18. What is the most important context from these news items that I should bring up naturally in an interview?

---

## THE STRATEGY

### Mission

19. What is the company's mission statement? How does it live up to this mission? Be specific with examples.

### Strategy

20. What do you think is the company's strategy?
21. Are there any missteps with respect to that strategy?

### Strengths

22. What are the product's selling points?
23. How does the company leverage those selling points?
24. What about the company or its products has enabled its success?

### Weaknesses

25. What are the major issues with the company and its products?
26. How should the company address those weaknesses, or should they just accept them?

### Challenges

27. What are the biggest challenges for the company right now?
28. How do you see them addressing those challenges?
29. What challenges have they already overcome?

### Opportunities

30. Is there anything on the horizon (with technology or in their industry) that might create an opportunity for the company?

### Threats

31. Is there anything on the horizon which might threaten the company's success?

### Future

32. What do you think the future holds for this company?
33. Think about any new products or features that would be a natural fit.

---

## THE CULTURE

### Culture

34. What is the company's culture? Look at what candidates, current employees, and former employees say about working there (Glassdoor, Blind, LinkedIn, Reddit).

### Values

35. What does the company value? Read interviews with the founders and think about their culture and products. (e.g., "move fast" at Facebook, "don't be evil" at Google)

### History

36. How long has the company been around?
37. How did the company get started? What is the founding story?
38. Have they been doing what they set out to do, or have they pivoted?

### Interviewers

39. If I share who will be interviewing me, what should I look for about them online? (LinkedIn, talks, blog posts — DO NOT message or stalk them)

### Key People

40. Who founded the company? What is their background?
41. What were the founders doing previously?
42. If it's a startup, who funded the company? Key investors and amounts.
43. Are there any well-known people at the company?

### Organization

44. How big is the company? (Employees, offices, global presence)
45. How is the company organized? (Teams, divisions, business units)
46. Does everyone report to the CEO or is there a strict hierarchy?

---

## THE ROLE

### Role

47. What does this role look like at this company? How is it defined here compared to other companies?
48. How technical is the role?
49. How do decisions get made? (Data-driven? Consensus? Executive decree?)

### Idea Generation

50. Where do ideas come from? Is the company "bottom up" (ideas bubble up from teams who convince executives) or "top down" (executives set the vision, teams execute)?

### Things to Change

51. What are 3 specific things I could suggest changing or implementing at the company? Use common user complaints and product gaps as a starting point.

### Why I Want the Job

52. Help me articulate why I should be excited about this company and this role. Give me specific, genuine reasons — not generic ones.

### Why I Would Be a Good Fit

53. Based on the company's challenges and the role requirements, make a compelling pitch for why I would be a good fit. Consider the company's current challenges and how my background could help. Go beyond the job description.
